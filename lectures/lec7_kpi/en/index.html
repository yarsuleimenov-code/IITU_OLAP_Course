II. KPI Conflicts and End-to-End Integration

Local KPI optimization leads to a "war of departments" and global sabotage. OLAP solves this problem through End-to-End KPIs, integrated into one model.

Typical KPI Conflicts

Sales (Volume) VS Finance (Margin)

Sales grants a discount to meet their volume target → Finance cannot achieve the planned Margin.

Procurement (Price) VS Production (Quality)

Procurement chooses the cheapest supplier → Production receives low-quality raw materials, increasing Defects.

Marketing (Promotion) VS Logistics (Timeliness)

Marketing launches a promotion → Logistics fails to ship the goods on time → OTD suffers.

HR (Hiring Speed) VS Managers (Qualification)

HR fills vacancies quickly → Managers receive unsuitable candidates.

V. IBP BEST PRACTICE: 3 Principles for the Analyst

KPIs are not just formulas, but strategic management tools embedded in the data architecture. These principles are critical for designing OLAP-based KPI systems.

1. Cascading (Top-Down)

KPIs are structured hierarchically: from Strategy (ROI, EBITDA) down to Operations (OTD). OLAP (Roll-Up) ensures instantaneous aggregation of operational data to the strategic level.

2. End-to-End KPIs (TCO)

Solves the "war of departments" by motivating toward overall profit. Use TCO (Total Cost of Ownership) or Adjusted LTV, linking multiple functional blocks in one metric.

3. Flexibility (Rolling Forecast)

The KPI system must support Rolling Forecast and Scenario Analysis (Worst/Best Case). KPIs are measured not only by Actuals but also by the up-to-date Plan.
