Integrated Business Planning (IBP)

From bureaucratic chaos to a unified strategy

I. What is IBP?

1. Definition

IBP is a monthly cyclical process that synchronizes all functional plans of a company (sales, production, finance) with a single strategic goal.

2. Core Principle

Transitioning from disparate plans ("sales plan" vs. "production plan") to a single, aligned plan (One Set of Numbers) that becomes law for the entire organization.

3. Difference from S&OP

IBP expands S&OP (Sales & Operations Planning) by including financial analysis and scenario modeling. S&OP focuses on volume, IBP focuses on value and profit.

II. The Problem: "War of the Departments" in Classic Planning

Without a unified plan, each department works toward its own goals, leading to conflicts, product shortages, and excess inventory. IBP closes these gaps.

III. Old Approach vs. The New IBP Standard

Classic Budgeting

  • ✗ Focus: Financial control
  • ✗ Horizon: Static, 1 year
  • ✗ Reaction to Change: Slow, once a year
  • ✗ Result: Plan quickly becomes outdated

Integrated Planning (IBP)

  • ✓ Focus: Optimizing profit and resources
  • ✓ Horizon: Rolling Forecast, 18-36 months
  • ✓ Reaction to Change: Fast, monthly cycle
  • ✓ Result: Current and flexible plan
